Overview


Context

In this UX design case study, we delve into the development of a game-changing public transport app, which addresses the glaring gap in the existing market - while numerous similar tools cater to local users, none effectively address the unique needs of tourists and travellers.



Problem

Even with public transport apps currently available in the market, travellers find public transport abroad confusing due to the language barrier and lack of general knowledge on how public transport is used in a given country. This makes navigating public transport difficult and stressful, diverting focus from enjoying the trip itself to the fullest.



Solution

Hopper is an innovative public transport app, designed to revolutionize the way travellers navigate cities around the world. What sets our app apart from competitors is its unique focus on cultural differences and public transport etiquette in each country.

Design Process



The design thinking process was used in this UX case study to ensure a user-centered and empathetic approach to solving design challenges. By using design thinking, I was aiming to deeply understand the needs, desires, and pain points of target users.

The process involved multiple stages, starting with empathizing with users to gain insights into their experiences and motivations. This was followed by defining the problem statement, ideating potential solutions, prototyping and testing them with users.

The iterative nature of the design thinking process allowed for continuous refinement and improvement of the solutions based on user input. Ultimately, the adoption of design thinking facilitated the development of innovative and effective UX designs that truly met the users' needs and expectations.
